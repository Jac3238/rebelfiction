EL DORADO


Reportedly, the big bad Kentucky bourbon industry produces 95% of the world’s supply. Regarding that other 5%, is there any bourbon not distilled and bottled in Kentucky that is even worthy of the prestigious classification of bourbon whiskey?

The ABCs of Bourbon:
A: ALCOHOL - No matter how hard the white dog bites after distillation, bottled bourbon cannot come out of the barrel exceeding 80 proof
B: BARREL - All bourbon must stored in virgin, charred, bona fide American oak barrels
C: CORN - Bourbon mash must contain 51% corn 

Renowned distillers such as Four Roses, Jim Beam, Maker’s Mark, Woodford Reserve, Buffalo Trace and Wild Turkey employ nearly 20,000 people and flood almost $9 billion annually into their old Kentucky home economy.

BIG BAD LOVE

His name was Koned, or “Horse Fly with One Wing”, but they called him “Smooth Talker”.
He could get anyone to do anything he wanted, but he didn’t know what he wanted.
From the red creeks at Antietam, to the glaze green cathedrals of the Wrong Wrong Valley, the men in his family had soldiered up and come home broken,
trailing homesick yankee brides in their wake. One shell shocked uncle had imported kudzu to the south as a promise to end soil erosion and maybe a feed crop to boot.
But it swallowed the land and the livestock wouldn’t touch it. Now, his house had been engulfed by it. His family lost, all of them searching for each other blind among the vines.
“If I ever escape,” he thought, “I think I’ll go to Mexico and join the circus.”

 

Deep in the heart of Dixieland, there is a real issue of combating natural or alcohol-induced hunger around the clock. What in tarnation does one eat when they are up to absolutely no good after having one-too-many? I’d bet pennies on the dollar that most restaurants close well before the bars on the town square let out.

If you find yourself cruising along anywhere along the Mighty Mississippi, the gas station is probably the only damn thing open after midnight.

How many beers does it take to romanticize a big ole Styrofoam box of the hottest tamales, spiciest crispitos, cheesiest jalapeño poppers, deep fried bangin chicken on a stick, or a succulent, sweet & sour spicy spring roll?

*But remember that the level of intoxication has the potential to inhibit one’s ability to accurately savor the flavor.

One of the greatest and most convenient aspects of a gas station grill is the ability to fill up the tank, wash off the windshield, pick up a pack of White Owl White Grape™ cigarillos, a case of Miller High Life™ and a little somethin’ somethin’ for the long ride home.

But all jokes aside: if you didn’t ask for dill pickle salt, extra sauce and something to drink, you better turn yourself right back around... You’re gonna end up high, dry and absolutely lost without sauce.   

While there are many different, individually owned gas stations across the region, almost all of these hidden gems don't even advertise. Even better, if you're in the right place at the right time, you might even get a deal on the leftovers.

“There ain’t nothing like it. It’s giving me all the feels just to think about James’ Food Corner at the BP over there off Jackson Avenue. Could somebody loan me a dime?“ said the local drunk. "I'm fixin to butter a biscuit and get my munch on, bubba."

No matter how inebriated, stoned or faded you are, there’s always some characters hanging out, loitering out front too. The gas station is as good as the local watering hole, a real hotspot to meet up with friends, snag a bag of grass from the dealer or wash off the gunk from your windshield after a late night drive to the county line.

Oh my, sweet apple pie.
